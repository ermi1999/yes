Data collected Q1 2026 from 214 employers across Addis Ababa, Hawassa, and Dire Dawa. Sample covers 8,427 individual positions across 12 sectors. All figures in Ethiopian Birr (ETB) per month unless noted. Exchange rate at time of publication: 1 USD = 126.4 ETB.

## Executive Summary

Ethiopia's labor market is in a period of unprecedented transformation. The launch of the Ethiopian Securities Exchange, the expansion of the digital economy, and a growing pipeline of foreign direct investment have created **acute demand** for skilled professionals — and compensation is responding.

The story of 2026 is not just rising wages — it's **divergence**. While entry-level salaries grew a modest 8.3%, senior management and specialist roles surged 24–31%, driven by an acute shortage of experienced professionals in finance, technology, and compliance.

## What This Means for Employers

### 1. Budget for 15–20% salary growth in 2027

The market is not cooling. Companies that freeze compensation at 2025 levels will lose talent to competitors and to the growing remote-work economy.

### 2. CISI certification commands a premium

Professionals with CISI qualifications are earning **35–50% more** than non-certified peers in equivalent roles. As ESX regulation tightens, this premium will increase.

### 3. Benefits are now a retention tool, not a perk

Medical insurance is table stakes. The differentiators are remote flexibility, education support, and equity participation. Companies offering all three report **40% lower turnover**.

<Callout type="warning" title="Data Limitations">
This survey covers formal-sector employment in urban centers. It does not capture informal employment, which represents an estimated 70–80% of Ethiopia's total workforce. Rural compensation patterns differ significantly from the figures presented here.
</Callout>
